Biography

I am a Ph.D. researcher in Paleobiology dedicated to understanding the complex interplay between life and its environment over deep time. My academic journey began with a strong foundation in Geology, leading to specialized research in the paleobiology of marine invertebrates.

I combine rigorous field sampling, paleocommunity analysis, and statistical modeling to reconstruct ancient marine ecosystems. My work aims to understand the drivers of evolutionary innovation, extinction, and community assembly, particularly across the Neogene-Quaternary intervals.

Research Interests

  • Macroecology: Analyzing large-scale patterns of biodiversity and distribution.
  • Macroevolution: Understanding origination and extinction rates among marine invertebrates.
  • Marine Paleoecology: Reconstructing ancient benthic communities and ecosystem functioning.
  • Predator-Prey Dynamics: Investigating drilling predation in deep time to trace the escalation of biotic interactions.
