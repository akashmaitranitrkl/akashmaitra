Investigating macroecological and macroevolutionary patterns in marine invertebrates and their causal relationships with biotic and abiotic drivers across the Neogene-Quaternary intervals.

Research Summary

My research focuses on understanding the evolutionary dynamics of marine benthic communities over deep time. By analyzing fossil records, particularly from the Neogene and Quaternary periods, I aim to uncover how major environmental perturbations and climatic shifts have shaped biodiversity and extinction rates.

A significant portion of my work investigates predator-prey dynamics, specifically focusing on drilling predation by Naticid gastropods. Through rigorous field sampling, paleocommunity analysis, and statistical modeling, I reconstruct ancient marine ecosystems to trace the escalation of biotic interactions and their role in macroevolution.
