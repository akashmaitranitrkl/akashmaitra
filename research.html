Research Themes

My research explores the macroevolutionary and macroecological dynamics of marine invertebrates over deep time.

[Marine Paleoecology]

Marine Paleoecology

Reconstructing ancient marine communities to understand community assembly, species interactions, and ecosystem functioning through time.

[Reef Ecosystems]

Reef Ecosystems

Analyzing Pleistocene coral reefs and associated mollusc assemblages to decipher paleoenvironmental conditions and reef development.

[Ecological Networks]

Ecological Networks

Applying network theory to paleocommunities to assess structural robustness, food web stability, and biotic interactions before and after major extinction events.

[Macroevolution]

Macroevolution

Investigating large-scale patterns of biodiversity, origination, and extinction rates among marine invertebrates to detect deep-time evolutionary trends.

[Plio-Pleistocene]

Plio-Pleistocene Biodiversity

Examining faunal responses to the extreme climatic fluctuations that characterized the recent geological past as an analogue for modern climate change.

[Mass Extinctions]

Mass Extinctions

Studying the causes, selectivity, and recovery patterns associated with major biotic crises in Earth's history.
