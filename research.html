Research Themes

My research explores the macroevolutionary and macroecological dynamics of marine invertebrates over deep time.

[Marine Paleoecology Image]

Marine Paleoecology

Reconstructing ancient marine communities to understand community assembly, species interactions, and ecosystem functioning through time. By examining the fossil record, we can determine how benthic communities responded to past environmental changes.

[Macroevolution Image]

Macroevolution

Investigating large-scale patterns of biodiversity, origination, and extinction rates among marine invertebrates. This involves analyzing massive fossil datasets to detect trends that are invisible at smaller temporal scales.

[Predation Image]

Predator-Prey Dynamics

Analyzing fossil evidence of drilling predation (e.g., Naticid gastropods) to trace the escalation of biotic interactions. These "trace fossils" provide direct evidence of ecological interactions in deep time.

[Biodiversity Image]

Plio-Pleistocene Biodiversity

Examining faunal responses to the extreme climatic fluctuations that characterized the recent geological past. This period serves as a critical analogue for understanding how modern marine ecosystems might respond to current climate change.

[Network Image]

Ecological Networks

Applying network theory to paleocommunities to assess structural robustness and food web stability before and after major extinction events.
